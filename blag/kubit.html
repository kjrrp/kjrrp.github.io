Say hello to my little friend

I am pleased to welcome a new member in the family.
Say hello to Kubit :

Looks nice.
It's a compact (but powerful) gaming computer, sitting right next to my TV in the living room.
Following my naming tradition (5 letters word, starting with a 'K'), I settled on the name Kubit because:
 1) It obviously looks like a cube
 2) It's a pun on the qubit, the equivalent of a bit in quantum computing
 3) It can be confused with this cubit

Build

Because I've been buiding my own computers since the late 90s, I bought my own hardware instead of buying the tower pre-built in a shop.
Here is the list of parts I used:

 Case: Cooler Master Elite 110
 Motherboard: MSI B85I
 Processor: Intel Core i5-4590s
 RAM: G.SKILL Ripjaw (2x 8GB)
 GPU: MSI Geforce GTX 760 Mini-ITX 2G
 Storage: PNY SSD 128GB
 Storage: Western Digital SSD 64GB
 Storage: Maxtor HDD 640GB
 PSU: akasa Venom Power 550 (Semi Modular)
 Water cooling: Cooler Master Seidon 120M
 Air cooling: LEPA BOL.QUIET 12cm

I'm always impress by how something so little can pack so much power. Then again I *do* have gigantic hands!

"Et là, c'est le drame..."

Of course the first time I pressed the power button the computer was not working. It couldn't pass the POST at all.
I use the technique take-everything-out-one-by-one-until-it-works (Patent pending) util I found out the root cause.

Uh oh...
It was a RAM module improperly plugged...

Conclusion

Everything is working fine now, Kubit is sitting silently (thank you water cooling) next to my TV.

Purring like a cat.
