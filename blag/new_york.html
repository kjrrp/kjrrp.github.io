New York in 6 hours


When I went to Argentina on a whim (Yeah I do that sometimes), I chose my flight back to have a layover in New York instead of a direct flight for two reason: 1) It was way (way) less expensive: 600€ instead of 2000€... and 2) My flight from Buenos Aires would land in JFK at 8AM while the flight to Paris would take off from Newark at 6PM. This means that I would have to go through Manhattan and accounting for customs and travel times, it would give me 6 hours in New York.
Having never been to New York in my life (strange, I know) I took this opportunity to challenge myself and see the most New York-y things in my short alloted time. I shared my project with friends and family who have been there; they all laughed at me. Let's see who had the final laugh...
Starting (and ending) at Penn Station where I left my luggage, I took a stroll in the Big Apple:

Conclusion

There you have it, a "typical" New York experience, albeit condensed in time. I didn't take any cab, subway or bus (In hindsight, I should have taken a Citi Bike) because only by walking can I really feel like I'm in a city. I rarely take public transportation when visiting a city, I really enjoy walking and losing my way in a city to discover it.
Following this urban trek I took the train to Newark airport and arrived at my gate with minutes to spare (I like to live dangerously).
I will come back to New York eventually, but a lot of what I would see will be "been there, done that".
